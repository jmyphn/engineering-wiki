# Mechanical Engineering Major

## [Aerospace Engineering üöÄ]({{ '/mae-aero/' | relative_url }})
Field of study focused on the design, analysis, and mechanics of airborne and spaceborne vehicles. Covers aerodynamics, propulsion (aircraft and rockets), orbital mechanics, and lightweight composite structures.

Career paths: Aerospace engineer, propulsion engineer, flight dynamics analyst, structures engineer, and mission systems architect.

## [Robotics & Mechatronics ü§ñ]({{ '/mae-robotics/' | relative_url }})
Interdisciplinary field combining mechanical systems with electronics and software control. Focuses on autonomous mobile robots, feedback control systems, sensors/actuators, and machine learning applied to physical systems.

Career paths: Robotics engineer, controls engineer, mechatronics specialist, automation engineer, and autonomous systems developer.

## [Energy & Thermal-Fluids ‚ö°]({{ '/mae-energy/' | relative_url }})
Field of study on the generation, conversion, and transfer of energy. Covers thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and advanced applications in renewable energy (wind, fusion), combustion, and multiphase systems.

Career paths: Thermal engineer, energy systems analyst, CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) engineer, HVAC design, and power plant engineer.

## [Automotive & EV Engineering üèéÔ∏è]({{ '/mae-auto/' | relative_url }})
Specialized study on the dynamics and propulsion of ground vehicles. Focuses on vehicle stability, electric powertrains, internal combustion, and the integration of IoT/sensors for modern connected vehicles.

Career paths: Automotive engineer, powertrain engineer, vehicle dynamics specialist, test engineer, and product development for EVs.

## [Materials & Manufacturing üî¨]({{ '/mae-materials/' | relative_url }})
Field of study connecting the atomic structure of matter to macroscopic mechanical properties and production. Covers composite materials, additive manufacturing (3D printing), digital manufacturing, and product design.

Career paths: Materials engineer, manufacturing engineer, product design engineer, R&D scientist, and quality assurance specialist.

## [Biomechanics ü¶¥]({{ '/mae-biomech/' | relative_url }})
Application of mechanical engineering principles to biological systems. Focuses on soft tissue mechanics, structural analysis of metabolic systems, and computer-aided engineering for medical devices and biological processes.

Career paths: Biomedical engineer, medical device designer, clinical engineer, and biomechanics researcher.

## [Systems Engineering üåê]({{ '/mae-systems/' | relative_url }})
Field of study on managing complexity in large-scale engineering projects. Covers system architecture, model-based systems engineering (MBSE), reliability, optimization, and decision-making frameworks.

Career paths: Systems engineer, operations analyst, project manager, reliability engineer, and technical program manager.
